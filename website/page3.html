Padua High Classics

This page will have outfits that make references to some of the characters/quotes of the movie 10 Things I Hate About You. Each outfit will be linked to an external site where users can buy said clothing, and some songs that match the outfits.





The "Angry Girl” Look

This look is inspired by Kat Stratford's signature dark, utilitarian style that implies an "I don't care" attitude and a dedication to something deeper than high school trends. This is the look for the days when you're too busy to be bothered with anything. Every piece is meant to prioritize function over trends, channeling Kat's morals

Featured Items:



  • Fitted Baby Tee (or crop top)
  • Dark Wash Cargo Pants
  • Plaid or Striped Flannel (worn unbuttoned)
  • Black Combat Boots
  • Padlock Necklace

    • Featured Songs:

  • "Window Pane" by Rusted Root
  • "Doll Parts" by Hole




    • The "You Don't Have To Buy Me Stuff" Look (Bianca)

    This outfit perfectly captures Bianca's teenage dilemma: wanting to look effortlessly cool for the popular crowd while still maintaining her signature feminine flair. It’s her post-makeover, slightly rebellious moment, signaling she's ready to challenge the status quo (and her father's rules). The look is flirty, a little daring, and screams mid-'90s high-school aspirational.

    Featured Items:



  • Ribbed White or Pastel Crop Sweater (slim fit, short length)
  • Dark Plaid or Houndstooth Mini Skirt (A-line or slightly pleated)
  • Sheer Black Tights (optional, for a cooler day)
  • Chunky Platform Loafers or Mary Janes.
  • Silver Butterfly Clips (worn in the hair)

    • Featured Songs:

  • "Just A Girl" by No Doubt
  • "Hit Me Baby Pne More Time" by Britney Spears
  • "Lovefool" by The Cardigans




    • The "I Hate the Way I Don't Hate You" Poet

    This style captures Kat Stratford at her most raw and vulnerable, the moment she performs her iconic poem. It moves away from aggressive punk gear and leans into a softer, yet still defiantly dark, academic grunge aesthetic. This look is a study in conflicted emotions, using a layered silhouette to hint at the guarded intellectual beneath the tough exterior. It's the uniform for the girl who carries a well-read copy of The Bell Jar and feels everything intensely. The outfit hinges on a classic late '90s layering trick: the casualization of formal wear.

    Featured Items:



  • Solid Fitted Black Long-Sleeve T-Shirt
  • Dark Velvet or Corduroy Slip Dress (simple, straight-cut, worn over the shirt)
  • Sleek Lace-Up Boots (less chunky than the combat style)
  • Simple Wirre-Rimmed Glasses

    • Featured Songs:

  • "Heart of Glass" by Blondie
  • "That's What You Get" by Paramore
